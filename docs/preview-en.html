1. Introduction: The Evolution of IBA Codification (1961-2024)

Mixology, understood as the intersection between chemical-physical science, the art of hospitality, and the sociology of consumption, has experienced an unprecedented formal structuring process in the last century. The core of this regulatory ecosystem is the International Bartenders Association (IBA). From its foundation and the first historic codification approved in Oslo in 1961 — which originally included 50 fundamental recipes — the IBA has assumed the role of guardian of the global liquid heritage.

The most recent update, formalized between 2020 and early 2024, represents a crucial turning point. The catalog has been expanded and meticulously rebalanced to include exactly 102 cocktails, divided into three perfectly symmetrical categories of 34 recipes each: “The Unforgettables”, “Contemporary Classics” and “New Era Drinks”.

2. Operational Analysis of High-Volume Service

2.1 The Mathematical Model 60/140/100

To standardize the service of these beverages while ensuring an acceptable palatal experience and rigorous control over alcohol intoxication, the industry adopts a rigid volumetric model based on a 300 ml highball glass:

  • ◆ 100 ml — Ice (lowers temperature, slows secondary dilution)
  • ◆ 60 ml — Alcoholic base (spirit or liqueur)
  • ◆ 140 ml — Non-alcoholic filler (soda, cola, energy drink, juice)

The result is a cocktail with an initial alcohol content of 12% ABV, equivalent to a glass of table wine — ideal for controlled consumption in high-volume contexts.

3. The IBA 2024 Encyclopedia

While the 60/140 volumetric model dominates the entertainment sector, professional high-mixing circles rely on the millimetric rigor established by the IBA. This corpus breaks down and critically analyzes each category, providing official recipes expressed in milliliters.

Category 1 — The Unforgettables

34 cocktails · the foundations of classic mixology

The “Unforgettables” section serves as the archaeological foundation of mixology. The 34 cocktails here are historical monuments, mostly created between the mid-nineteenth century and the decade following the American Prohibition (1933). Organoleptically, these drinks are defined as “spirit-forward”: the structure relies on the exaltation of the base distillate.

🍸 Negroni

✦ Ingredients

30 ml Gin
30 ml Bitter Campari
30 ml Sweet Red Vermouth

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Build in Old Fashioned glass with ice. Stir. Garnish: Orange slice.

🍸 Daiquiri

✦ Ingredients

60 ml White Cuban Rum
20 ml Lime Juice
2 b.s. Superfine Sugar

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Stir to dissolve sugar. Shake with ice. Strain into cocktail glass. Garnish: None.

🍸 Dry Martini

✦ Ingredients

60 ml Gin
10 ml Dry Vermouth

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Stir in mixing glass. Strain into Martini glass. Garnish: Olive or lemon zest.

🍸 Manhattan

✦ Ingredients

50 ml Rye Whiskey
20 ml Sweet Red Vermouth
1 dash Angostura

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Stir in mixing glass. Strain into cocktail glass. Garnish: Maraschino cherry.

🍸 Old Fashioned

✦ Ingredients

45 ml Bourbon or Rye
1 sugar cube
2 dash Angostura
Splash water

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Muddle sugar and bitters. Add ice and whiskey. Garnish: Zest and cherry.

🍸 Whiskey Sour

✦ Ingredients

45 ml Bourbon
25 ml Lemon
20 ml Syrup
20 ml Egg White (opt.)

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Shake with ice. Strain. Garnish: Orange and cherry.

🥃 Cuba Libre

✦ Ingredients

50 ml White Rum
120 ml Cola
10 ml Lime Juice

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Build in highball with ice. Garnish: Lime wedge.

🍸 Cosmopolitan

✦ Ingredients

40 ml Citron Vodka
15 ml Cointreau
15 ml Lime
30 ml Cranberry

✦ Procedure & Garnish

Shake with ice. Strain into cocktail glass. Garnish: Lemon twist.
